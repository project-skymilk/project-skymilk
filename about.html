About Sky Milk


The United States has initiated an exodus of troops from West Africa in the midst of an onslaught of extremist attacks pushing into Burkina Faso, Niger, and other countries in the Sahel region. As Pentagon counterterrorism operations shift towards threats originating from China and Russia, insurgents that threaten overlooked areas are deemed of lesser relevance to national security. However as history has shown, often these unsubdued insurgents snowball into increasingly unstoppable terrorist cells.

Built on Amazon Lightsail, Sky Milk is storing satellite imagery of suspect areas daily and applying statisical classification towards elements in the imagery. Based on training data from previous projects such as Satellite Sentinel Project, we hope to automate the process of land-use monitoring and trigger alerts upon suspicious activity. After human and algorithmic inspection, Sky Milk will release detailed findings and attempt to issue warnings to relevant agencies should they be unaware of the situation.
